The questions I'd expect on the call

Everything else in this case study argues a position from outside the company. This page flips the seat. If I were prepping Croda's CFO for the next earnings call, here is what I think analysts would actually press on, and the lines I'd want ready. A couple of these are uncomfortable on purpose, including the one my own valuation model raises against my own thesis.

01

"You've just written off £45m on the Lamar lipid site and put it on standby. Doesn't that undercut the idea that demand for this platform is durable beyond COVID?"

Talking point

No, and the distinction is footprint versus demand. The capacity at Lamar was sized for a vaccine cycle that was never going to be the steady state. Consolidating into fewer, more efficient sites while group capex comes down from £137.9m to £108.2m is the capital discipline investors have been asking for, not a retreat from the platform. Avanti Research signed a new lipid partnership with Certest Biotec in March 2025. We are still building this out, just not everywhere at once.

02

"Crop Protection fell 8% in the first quarter. Is destocking actually finished, or are you just hoping it is?"

Talking point

The comparator matters here. Last year's first quarter included customers restocking ahead of an expected shortage, and that doesn't repeat. What I can commit to is a transparent read every quarter rather than a number I can't yet stand behind. The order book is healthy and slightly ahead of our own plan, which is the leading indicator I'd point you to before reported growth catches up with it.

03

"You're guiding to an adjusted operating margin above 20% by 2028, up from 17.4% today. Walk me through what actually has to go right."

Talking point

Three things, none of them heroic alone. Working capital comes down by roughly £50m structurally. Capex normalises to about 6% of sales as the heavy investment period ends. And the mix keeps shifting toward Consumer Care and patented ingredients, which already carry a better margin than the group average. It isn't one bet, it's three smaller ones that have to land in the same direction.

04

"Your payout ratio is 76%, well above your own 40 to 50% policy. Is the dividend at risk?"

Talking point

It depends which earnings line you're using. On reported IFRS EPS, which absorbed this year's exceptional impairments, the ratio looks stretched. On adjusted EPS, which is what the policy was actually set against, it reads differently. Our intention is to grow earnings back into the policy band, not to cut the dividend to fit a ratio.

05 — the hard one

"I've run a straightforward discounted cash flow off your own guidance and I get a value below where the stock trades today. Why should I still be a buyer?"

Talking point

Because a five year DCF only prices what's already in consensus, and the lipid platform's growth beyond vaccines, gene therapy, oncology, rare disease, isn't in consensus yet. I'd rather be straight about this than spin it: my own model gives a base case a touch below the current price too. The wide range either side of that base case is the honest signal, not the single number. The thesis here was never "the DCF says buy." It's that the market is still pricing COVID's hangover and not yet pricing what replaced it.

06

"ROIC is 8.2% today against a target of over 10% by 2028. Most multi-year targets like that get missed. Why believe this one?"

Talking point

Because it isn't an aspiration, it's arithmetic. The two biggest levers, capex discipline and working capital, are the same two already moving and already disclosed. We reiterated the target at the first quarter rather than walking it back. Judge us on the interim milestones, not just the 2028 number.

One thing I'd flag before anyone asks

The GLP-1 obesity drug story shows up in almost every recent lipid nanoparticle headline, and a journalist or analyst could easily assume Croda benefits from it directly. We don't, not from the oral pills, they use a different delivery technology entirely. Better to correct that assumption on our own terms now than have an analyst discover the gap later and wonder what else might have been overstated.

Sanjeeban Sumans. June 2026.
